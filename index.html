Sematic tags

semantic elements clearly define their content.
For instance, <form>, <table>, and <article> tags clearly define the content and purpose, to the browser.

Here are some of the fundamental HTML5 semantic elements that you should use to structure your web content:

  1. <article> – Represents an independent piece of content (like a blog post or news article).
  2. <aside> – Represents content that is tangentially related (like a sidebar).
  3. <details> – Used for content that can be expanded/collapsed by the user.
  4. <figcaption> – Caption/description for a figure.
  5. <figure> – Used to group media (images, diagrams, code snippets) with captions.
  6. <footer> – Represents the footer of a page or section (copyright, contact info).
  7. <header> – Represents the header of a page or section (introductory content, navigation).
  8. <main> – Represents the main content area of the page.
  9. <mark> – Used to highlight or mark text.
  10. <nav> – Represents a section of navigation links.
  11. <section> – Represents a thematic grouping of content (like chapters, or major divisions).
